## Code Fix: Eckart Projection for n_neg

**File:** `src/gadplus/search/gad_search.py`

The convergence check counts `n_neg` (number of negative Hessian eigenvalues).
Per CLAUDE.md, this MUST be on the **Eckart-projected vibrational Hessian**.

Previously, when `use_projection=False` (pure GAD), `n_neg` was counted on the
raw 3N×3N Hessian — which always has ≥6 negative eigenvalues (translation/rotation
modes). Convergence was impossible.

**Fix:** Always call `vib_eig()` for eigenvalue counting, regardless of `use_projection`.
The flag now only controls whether the GAD *vector computation* uses projected dynamics.

**File:** `src/gadplus/core/mode_tracking.py`

`pick_tracked_mode()` crashed when `k=0` and `v_prev is not None` (empty tensor argmax).
**Fix:** When `k=0`, always return lowest eigenvector (no tracking), with sign continuity.

### Key Findings

1. **Pure GAD works excellently at 0pm noise** (98% convergence, avg 10 steps).
   This validates the algorithm and Eckart projection are correct.

2. **Sharp falloff with noise:** 24% at 20pm, 6% at 40pm, 0% at ≥60pm.
   The effective working range of pure GAD is <20pm (~0.2 Å).

3. **Reactant starts never find TS** (all n_neg=0, force<0.001 — converged to minimum).
   GAD from equilibrium geometry descends, doesn't ascend to saddle point.

4. **Product starts** get 8% — slightly better because some products are closer to TS.

5. **Midpoint R→T** gets 18% — geometry between reactant and TS is a better starting point.
   36/41 failures are "almost_converged" → more steps would help.

6. **Failure modes shift with noise:**
   - Low noise (0–20pm): mostly "almost_converged" (need more steps/smaller dt)
   - Medium noise (40–60pm): "genuinely_stuck" takes over (wrong eigenvector tracked)
   - High noise (≥80pm): dominated by "genuinely_stuck" (70%+) and "drifting"

7. **What higher feature levels should fix:**
   - Mode tracking (level 1): reduce "genuinely_stuck" by maintaining eigenvector continuity
   - Eckart projection in dynamics (level 2): better GAD vector in rotated frames
   - Adaptive dt (level 3): handle the large-eigenvalue regime at high noise
   - NR refinement (level 4): polish "almost_converged" cases to convergence
