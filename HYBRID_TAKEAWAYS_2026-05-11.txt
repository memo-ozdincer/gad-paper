Hybrid GAD-Newton benchmark — informal takeaways (2026-05-11)
=============================================================

Comprehensive points (the 5-6 ideas that carry the story):

- Plain GAD beats Sella on IRC chemistry validation at high noise
  (+11.9pp at 150pm; +21.3pp at 200pm); they tie below 50pm.

- Sella is the per-step champion: 4-13 median steps vs GAD's 100-740.
  But each Sella step is expensive, so it's not the wall-time winner.

- Hybrid GAD-Newton sits between in step count but lowest on wall:
  1.4-3x faster than Sella, 4-4.5x faster than plain GAD at every noise.

- Hybrid pays a small chemistry tax (1-6pp behind plain GAD on IRC TOPO)
  but inherits GAD's right-basin character; Sella's "wins" at high
  noise are often wrong-saddle and IRC rejects them.

- Hybrid produces the geometrically tightest converged TSs at high
  noise (p95 7.7x tighter than Sella, 4.2x tighter than plain GAD at
  200pm) - the only axis where hybrid beats plain GAD too.

- Recommended default: hybrid damped Eckart eig-switch tr=0.05. Wins
  wall at every noise; chemistry within a few pp of plain GAD; tightest
  geometries.


Auxiliary points (mechanism + diagnostic notes):

- Raw conv and IRC TOPO are two different things: a 65pp raw-conv
  chasm at 100pm collapses to 7pp on IRC TOPO. Strict-threshold
  failures are often in the right basin already.

- Newton firing in the hybrid has three regimes: never (no-Eckart
  Cartesian frame at default thresholds), sometimes (Eckart force-
  switch at low noise), always (Eckart eigenvalue-switch).

- The "no-Eckart hybrid" was never actually doing Newton at default
  thresholds - Cartesian ||F||_2 never reaches sf<=0.01. It's plain
  GAD with a trust-radius cap. Confirmed by placebo test: sf=1e-3 and
  sf=1e-2 give identical numbers.

- Eckart projection is necessary only for strict-geometry convergence.
  Cartesian Newton at sf=0.05 fires constantly, raw conv crashes to
  40%, but IRC TOPO is unchanged at 89%. Same basin, wrong pose.

- Damping helps raw conv by 0-2.1pp (grows with noise) but is null
  (+-0.4pp) on IRC TOPO at every noise. Use it - never hurts.

- Sella IRC recovery is often *negative*: at 10pm and >=150pm noise,
  IRC catches Sella's "converged" wrong saddles, so IRC TOPO < raw
  conv. GAD and hybrid recover positively (basin character preserved
  when raw conv fails).

- The hybrid trajectory decomposes cleanly: GAD walks (median 0-91
  steps to first-Newton across 10-200pm), then Newton lands (4-18
  extra steps regardless of noise). All noise scaling is in the GAD
  phase.

- Hybrid's wrong-saddle failure mode: 44% at 200pm (vs plain GAD's
  25%). Newton's mode-following misses when noise scrambles
  eigenvectors. IRC partly recovers (+5.6pp vs +1.4pp for plain GAD).

- TOPO is consistently ~2x more permissive than RMSD-intended (the
  strict 0.3A criterion). Same disagreement structure across all
  three families - not a method-specific artifact.

- Sella has variants: libdef (canonical, cart+Eckart), default (cart
  no-Eckart), internal (lib default, internal coords), and Hess-freq
  d=3 (Hessian every 3rd step). d=3 actually wins raw conv at low
  noise (96.5% @ 10pm vs 92.7% for our d=1) with the SAME total wall.

- The d=3 mechanism (CORRECTED): same total compute as d=1, but d=3
  produces more "false positive" convergences. d=3 IRC TOPO at 10pm is
  88.5% vs d=1's 89.2% (-0.7pp). The +3.8pp raw conv gain doesn't
  survive chemistry validation. Stale Hessian misjudges saddle
  character -> trust-region claims wrong-saddle convergences. Our
  project's d=1 ("fresh HIP H every step") was the right call.

- Sella's library default IS d=3 (every 3 steps). Our project's "libdef
  d=1" was a benchmark-driven choice for fresher Hessian. On raw conv
  d=3 wins by +3.8pp at 10pm, but on IRC TOPO d=1 wins by 0.7pp. The
  raw-conv chase is misleading; IRC validation is the honest metric.

- Hybrid's trust radius matters little within 0.005-0.10 (raw conv
  varies <=2pp). The trust-radius cap rarely bites once Newton fires;
  Newton's eigenvalue regularization is the effective trust region.

- Total compute spend for the hybrid work: ~200 GPU-hours over ~24h
  wall on a100_2g.10gb MIG slices. 48 cells across 7 SLURM submissions.
